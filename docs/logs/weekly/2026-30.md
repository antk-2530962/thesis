## 1. Findings so far

### 1.1 The ~73% plateau is **overfitting**, not underfitting (07-19)

The question TODO §7.1 was written to answer, answered on day 1. Train-vs-val accuracy at each
run's best epoch, over 5 runs spanning 3 architectures:

| arch / subset | train | val | gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| bilstm FP_118/xy | 0.9886 | 0.7526 | **0.236** |
| bilstm ME_126/xy | 0.9716 | 0.7503 | **0.221** |
| gru ME_126/xy | 0.9022 | 0.7472 | **0.155** |
| lstm ME_126/xy | 0.9225 | 0.7453 | **0.177** |

Every model memorizes 90–99% of the training set and generalizes to ~75%. The plateau task
list's own branch condition therefore fires: **augmentation and regularization (§7.4) before
new features**. A model memorizing 99% of its training data is not short of signal — the
normalization (§7.2) and motion (§7.3) work stays worth doing on its merits, but it is not
what is capping accuracy today.

### 1.2 The residual errors are semantic, not geometric (07-19)

Aggregated over 18 independently trained models: `awake→wake` 0.42, `mouth→lips` 0.31,
`give→gift` 0.30, `cut→scissors` 0.26, `nap→sleep` 0.21. Several are confused **symmetrically**
— the models cannot tell which of the two it is in *either* direction. That points at a
possible **label ceiling** (some of these signs may not be reliably distinguishable in this
data) rather than a feature deficiency, and the next diagnostic (§7.1 step 5) should be
allowed to return that verdict.

### 1.3 A 20-point "architecture result" was a misconfiguration

`cnn1d` scoring 0.54 against ~0.75 read as "1D-CNN is not in the same league". It wasn't the
architecture: `num_layers` had been flattened 5 → 2 while hand-syncing four notebooks'
hyperparameters, and for `CausalConv1D` that parameter *is* the receptive field —
**125 frames → 13**, on 128-frame sequences. Caught by diffing the working tree against `HEAD`
during the training consolidation, i.e. by the very change meant to prevent it.
**cnn1d must be re-run before any conclusion about it stands.**

### 1.4 A leaked native graph, not a Jupyter limitation

The first POPSIGN pilot stalled at 18/20 with **every worker at 0% CPU and no error**. Cause:
POPSIGN mixes resolutions, a resolution change raises `RET_CHECK` inside the persistent
landmarker, and the retry handler rebuilt it by rebinding `_LANDMARKER` **without `.close()`**
— leaking a native graph and ~70 threads each time. The wedged worker sat at 219 threads /
1.3 GB against 76 / 614 MB for its siblings. `imap_unordered` cannot detect a live-but-hung
worker, so an overnight 30K-video run would have hung silently.

This also retired a repeated belief: the "multiprocessing can't run in a Jupyter kernel"
guard, whose stated mechanism (*spawn re-imports `__main__`, which in a notebook is the
kernel*) is simply false — `main_path` is only set when `__main__` has a `__file__`, and a
kernel has none. Verified by running the pool in a real ipykernel: **4 videos, 2 workers, 0
failed, 21.6 s** — the same wall time as the CLI, through the exact sequence that had
deadlocked.

> **The lesson worth carrying:** a plausible mechanism attached to a real symptom is not a
> diagnosis. That story explained the observation, was repeated into four documents and a
> runtime guard, and was wrong — while the actual bug sat undetected until a pilot hung and
> somebody looked at the process table.

### 1.5 POPSIGN's quality proxies were measuring clip padding

Mean any-hand detection rate of 0.427 reads as "MediaPipe fails on more than half the frames".
It doesn't: hand presence peaks at **0.86** mid-clip and sits at 0.12–0.19 across the first and
last fifths; measured between the first and last hand-bearing frame the same extraction scores
**0.85 mean / 0.94 median**. `longest_gap_frames` correlates with `n_frames` at rho **0.84**.
The threshold effect being ranked (~0.02) is an order of magnitude below the artifact
contaminating the ranking (~0.4), so the confidence sweep is **paused at 2 of 7 arms** until
the proxies are restricted to the signing span.

Related and larger than any threshold: only **1.1%** of frames carry both hands, and
`hand_rate` tops out at exactly 0.50 across the sample — the signature of "exactly one hand,
always" — on a sample including two-handed ASL signs. No confidence threshold addresses that.
